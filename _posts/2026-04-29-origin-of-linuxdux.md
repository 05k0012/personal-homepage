People sometimes ask where the name LinuxDux.com comes from. The short answer is that it started at The Evergreen State College, and like most humorous things in tech, it began with a misunderstanding.

Back when I was working as a system administrator in the Evergreen IT department, one of the students told me they really liked the “Linux duck.” They were talking about Tux, the Linux mascot. For whatever reason, the idea that someone thought Tux was a duck struck all of us as funny. It became an inside joke almost immediately. Even though Tux is very much a penguin, the new name stuck. From that point on, Tux became the Linux duck.

As jokes tend to do, it kept going. Eventually I decided I wanted a domain name, and LinuxDux felt personal and memorable. It also turned out to be a bit of a burden. In the real world, having a custom domain can confuse people. It is surprisingly hard to spell out L I N U X D U X over the phone. I learned quickly that having a simple Gmail or Outlook address makes life easier because people expect it. A custom domain throws them off. In some ways that confusion is part of the charm. The domain is inexpensive to maintain, and it still makes me smile because of the story behind it.

There is even a tattoo involved. A friend of mine actually got a picture of Tux with the words “Linux Duck” tattooed on her arm. That is some ultimate nerd cred.

That is the origin story of LinuxDux.

My handle, oldschool or oskool or whatever variation ends up being available, has an equally simple beginning. In the Evergreen CTF team and computer security club, everyone needed a handle because that was the style at the time. This was a group that still used IRC for that classic hacker vibe. One day I walked into the lab and my buddy called me oldschool. I was almost 30 in undergrad, so the name fit. It stuck immediately, and ever since then I have used some form of oldschool on servers and message boards.

Unfortunately, it is not a very unique handle, which is why I ended up with variations like oskool or 05k007 or whatever else was available. The idea was that if you were in that small corner of the world at the time, the Evergreen computer science lab in 2018, you would know me as oskool. I signed administrative scripts and messages with that handle. It became part of my identity in that space.

So that is another small part of my origin story. LinuxDux and oskool both came from simple moments that stuck, and they have followed me ever since.
