## My Ode to Asparagus

### Discovering What Fresh Really Means

It is asparagus season, so I really want to do another blog post about asparagus and how much I love asparagus, mostly just fresh asparagus. Up until I read *Animal, Vegetable, Mineral* by Barbara Kingsolver, I had not realized what I was missing out on with fresh foods in general. I really did not understand how big the difference is in flavor, consistency, color, and all the little idiosyncrasies that come with asparagus being in season versus out of season.

Now that it is April, asparagus in Washington State is finally showing up at local farms and markets, and it feels like a small seasonal miracle every time I see it.

### A Bad Meal, a Train Ride, and a Mood Shift

A few weeks ago, I was in kind of a bummer mood. As I gracefully age, I have discovered that my mood is really affected by whether I get enough sleep, whether I eat well, whether I exercise, and whether I actually engage with my emotions. All four of those things have a huge impact on how I feel.

I had gone to a restaurant and eaten food that was really not good. I slept terribly, felt awful the next day, and knew exactly why. Luckily, the light rail extension to Redmond had just opened, so I decided to treat myself to a new experience. I got on the train, rode out to Redmond on a beautiful Saturday morning, walked around Marymoor Park, and enjoyed the nature and the quiet. It helped more than I expected.

### The First Asparagus of the Season

On my way home, I stopped at the Capitol Hill Farmers Market, and that was where I saw asparagus for the first time this season. Local fresh asparagus in season. It changed my whole mood. It brightened me up instantly. The long walk helped, but the asparagus sealed the deal.

If you have read other posts on this site, you know I am really into olive oil, and asparagus pairs incredibly well with it. I bought two bundles immediately and went home excited to cook.

### How I Cook It

My method is simple and reliable.

**Ingredients**

- Fresh asparagus
- Olive oil
- Salt

**Steps**

1. Cut off the gristly ends.
2. Spread the asparagus on a baking sheet.
3. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with salt.
4. Mix it all together so everything is coated.
5. Roast at 425 to 450 degrees for about 10 minutes.

It crisps up beautifully and comes out delicious every time. You can add broccolini to the pan too. Just make sure everything is spaced out evenly.

### Serving Ideas

Asparagus plates well with almost anything. In the photos for this post, you will see it paired with:

- Asparagus and rice
- Asparagus with pork chops
- Asparagus with steak
- Asparagus and broccolini served alongside tacos

For the tacos, I cooked chicken thighs, then deglazed the pan with white wine, mounted the sauce with butter, and added a little fresh salsa. It made a great pan sauce for the asparagus.

### A Short Season, A Big Impact

One thing to keep in mind is that asparagus is only fresh and local for a couple of months. After that, most of it comes from Mexico. That is fine with me because as May and June roll in, tomatoes start showing up, and I cannot wait for fresh summery tomatoes. It is a shame they are not in the same season. There is a little overlap, but not much. In-season tomatoes are so much better than winter tomatoes. That is a whole different blog post.

So this is my ode to asparagus.
