This post is focused on concrete, repeatable approaches to tacos at home. Recipes are organized from the base (tortillas) to fillings, then salsas and toppings. Each recipe exists because it solves a specific practical need in making tacos.

## 🫓 Homemade Tortillas

Tortillas are the foundation. Getting them right makes everything else easier.

### Ingredients

- 2 cups masa harina (corn flour for tortillas)
- 1.5 cups warm water
- Pinch of salt

### Process

1. Mix masa and salt in a bowl.
2. Add warm water slowly while mixing with your hand.
3. Knead until smooth. Dough should:
   - Be soft but not sticky
   - Hold together without cracking
4. Let rest 10–15 minutes.
5. Roll into golf ball-sized portions.
6. Line a tortilla press with plastic wrap, press each ball into a thin disk.
7. Heat a dry skillet or cast iron pan over medium-high.
8. Transfer tortilla to pan immediately. Cook 30–45 seconds until edges lift, flip, and cook another 30–60 seconds.

### Notes

- Dough too dry → cracks when pressing.
- Dough too wet → sticks to plastic or pan.
- Puffed tortilla indicates proper hydration and heat.

## 🌮 Sticky Taco Beef

This filling reproduces the slightly wet, cohesive texture of fast-food style taco meat.

### Ingredients

- 1 lb ground beef (80/20)
- 1 small potato, finely diced
- 1 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Pinch MSG
- Pinch cayenne
- 2–3 tbsp white vinegar

### Process

1. Brown beef in a pan over medium heat. Keep the fat.
2. Add diced potatoes, cook until soft.
3. Mix in spices.
4. Add vinegar and stir continuously until mixture thickens and holds together.

Result: moist, cohesive, and adheres to tortillas without falling apart.

## 🥩 Carne Asada Tips

Simple, high-flavor steak filling.

### Ingredients

- Flank or skirt steak
- Lime juice
- Orange juice
- Garlic
- Salt
- Oil

### Process

1. Marinate steak 1–4 hours.
2. Cook over very high heat; avoid overcrowding the pan.
3. Rest steak before slicing.
4. Slice against the grain for tenderness.

Tip: Thin cuts cook quickly and retain juiciness when rested.

## 🍔 Smashburger Taco

A playful variation that fuses burger and taco elements.

### Ingredients

- 1 tortilla
- 2–3 oz ground beef
- Cheese slice
- Shredded lettuce
- Tomato slices
- Pickles
- Ketchup and mustard

Optional sauce:
- 2 tbsp mayo
- 1 tbsp ketchup
- 1 tbsp relish

### Process

1. Heat pan and lightly warm tortilla. Flip once.
2. Add cheese to cooked side.
3. Press raw beef onto tortilla, smash thin.
4. Cook until browned. Flip so beef is on bottom, cook until crisp.
5. Top with lettuce, tomato, pickles, and optional sauce.

## 🌶️ Roasted Salsa

Adds acidity, sweetness, and depth to tacos.

### Ingredients

- 4 tomatoes
- 2 tomatillos
- 1 small onion
- 2 garlic cloves
- 1–3 peppers (jalapeño, serrano, or similar)
- Salt
- Optional: splash of vinegar or drizzle of olive oil

### Process

1. Rough chop ingredients.
2. Cook onions and peppers first in a hot pan. Add tomatoes and garlic later.
3. Roast until lightly charred.
4. Blend to desired consistency. Add salt to taste.

## 🧅 Fresh Salsa Option

Quick, bright alternative.

### Ingredients

- Tomato
- Onion
- Cilantro
- Lime
- Salt

### Process

1. Chop and mix. Serve immediately.

## Toppings

- Cilantro, diced white onion
- Mexican cheeses (queso fresco, cotija) or shredded cheddar
- Lettuce, tomato
- Pickled vegetables if available

Toppings are flexible. Adjust for texture, acidity, or richness as desired.

## Personal Notes

I started with store-bought crunchy shells and ground beef, shredded lettuce, and tomato. That meal worked because it was simple, consistent, and easy to hold. Over time I explored homemade tortillas, experimented with fillings, and discovered the sticky beef and smashburger variations. Salsa stayed constant as a bright, balancing element. Tacos gradually became a practice of small observations: how dough feels, how beef should hold together, how vegetables bring brightness. It is still about simplicity, repeatability, and a practical balance of flavors and textures.
